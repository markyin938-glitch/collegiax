## Brand & Style

This design system transitions the high-energy, technical aesthetic of a cyber-campus into a sophisticated, light-mode environment. The brand personality is intellectually rigorous yet vibrantly modern, targeting developers, students, and tech-forward professionals.

The style is **Modern Minimalist with Brutalist undertones**. It leverages heavy whitespace and a strict adherence to a grid, but replaces raw dark-mode textures with "Gallery-Grade" surfaces—ultra-clean, high-contrast, and structurally precise. The emotional response should be one of clarity, high-speed performance, and professional transparency. It maintains its "cyber" edge through distinctive typography and sharp, intentional accents rather than dark gradients or neon glows.

## Colors

The palette is anchored by a "Stark Light" philosophy. Surfaces use a strategic mix of pure white (#FFFFFF) for primary work areas and off-white (#F2F2F2) for structural depth and sidebars.

- **Primary:** Pure Black (#000000) provides the highest possible contrast for core branding and primary actions, ensuring the "Cyber" weight remains.
- **Secondary:** Electric Cobalt (#5D5DFF) serves as the functional accent for links, active states, and focus indicators.
- **Tertiary:** Digital Cyan (#00F0FF) is used sparingly for data visualization and high-energy highlights.
- **Neutrals:** A range of cool-toned grays creates hierarchy without introducing warmth, maintaining the professional "campus" feel.

## Typography

The typographic system pairs the expressive, avant-garde geometry of **Syne** for headings with the understated, functional clarity of **DM Sans** for interface and body text.

- **Syne** is set with tight tracking in display sizes to emphasize its architectural forms.
- **DM Sans** provides a neutral, highly readable foundation for technical content and dense information.
- All text is rendered in high-contrast #0A0A0A to ensure accessibility on white and off-white backgrounds.
- Use uppercase for labels and small metadata to maintain the "systemized" aesthetic.

## Layout & Spacing

This design system utilizes a **Rigid Grid** model inspired by technical blueprints and academic journals. 

- **Grid:** A 12-column fluid grid on desktop, shifting to a 4-column grid on mobile.
- **Spacing:** Based on a 4px baseline. Components use 8px, 16px, 24px, and 32px increments for internal padding.
- **Structural Dividers:** Instead of using wide gutters, the design system often employs 1px borders to separate content blocks, maximizing screen real estate for data-heavy views.
- **Margins:** Generous outer margins (48px+) on desktop create a gallery-like focus on the central content.

## Elevation & Depth

To maintain a clean, high-energy aesthetic, this design system avoids traditional shadows. Depth is communicated through **Tonal Layering and Sharp Outlines**.

- **Level 0 (Base):** #F8F9FA (Neutral gray background).
- **Level 1 (Cards/Sections):** #FFFFFF (Pure white) with a 1px #E2E2E2 border.
- **Level 2 (Popovers/Modals):** #FFFFFF with a 2px solid Black border and a "hard" 4px offset shadow in #E2E2E2 (no blur).
- **Active State:** Elements may use a subtle inner stroke of the Secondary color (#5D5DFF) to indicate focus or selection.

## Shapes

The shape language is **Sharp (0px)**. All containers, buttons, and inputs feature 90-degree corners. This reinforces the technical, "cyber" campus feel and differentiates the product from more consumer-oriented, soft-edged apps. The lack of border-radius demands precision in alignment and creates a sense of structural integrity and authority.

## Components

- **Buttons:** Primary buttons are solid Black (#000000) with White text and sharp corners. Secondary buttons are White with a 1px Black border. Hover states should invert the colors or introduce the Cobalt accent.
- **Input Fields:** Use an off-white (#F2F2F2) background with a bottom-only 2px Black border to emulate technical forms.
- **Chips:** Small, rectangular tags with #E2E2E2 backgrounds and uppercase DM Sans bold text.
- **Cards:** White surfaces, no shadows, 1px #E2E2E2 borders. Header sections within cards should have a light gray #F2F2F2 background to separate metadata from body content.
- **Code Blocks:** Use a slightly darker neutral gray (#F0F0F0) to distinguish technical snippets from standard body text, maintaining high legibility.
- **Checkboxes:** Square, sharp-edged, filling with solid Black and a white checkmark when active.
